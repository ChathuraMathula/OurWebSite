Derivative of a Function

❯❯

  • Consider a function \(y\ =\ f( x)\).

  • \(x\) is the independant variable

  • \(y\) is the dependant variable
(Because \(y\) depends on \(x\) such that it satisfies the conditions denoted by \(f\))
  • Firstly we will graph these data on an \(xy\) plane.

(This plane is called the Cartesian Plane in mathematics)
\(x\) - axis shows the \(x\) values and \(y\) - axis shows the \(f( x)\) values
  • We draw \(x\) against \(f(x)\)

Assume our graph looks like in figure 06
  • We will select a point on the graph to find the gradient (slope) of the graph at this point.

Gradient is also another term that simply tells the ratio of \(y\) to \(x\). that is \(y/x\)
  • We take another point as in figure 08 and draw a straight line through the two points

  • Next, we find the slope(=gradient) of the straight line as follows.

$$slope = \delta y/\delta x$$ (\(\delta\) is used to denote a very small amount)

$$slope\ =\frac{\ difference\ between\ y\ values}{\ difference\ between\ x\ values}$$

$$slope\ =\frac{f( a\ +\ \delta a) \ -\ f( a)}{\ ( a\ +\ \delta a) \ -\ a}$$

$$slope\ =\frac{f( a\ +\ \delta a) \ -\ f( a)}{\delta a}$$

  • Let's replace \(a\) with \(x\), because \(a\) can be any value in the domain \(x\)

$$slope\ =\frac{\delta y}{\delta x} \ =\frac{f( x\ +\ \delta x) \ -\ f( x)}{\delta x}$$

  • Then we find the limit of the function at both sides when \(\delta x\) approaches \(0\)

$$\lim\limits _{\delta x\rightarrow 0} \frac{\delta y}{\delta x} \ =\lim\limits _{\delta x\rightarrow 0}\frac{f( x\ +\ \delta x) \ -\ f( x)}{\delta x}$$

  • Let's assume this equals to \(L\)

$$\lim\limits _{\delta x\rightarrow 0}\frac{\delta y}{\delta x} \ =L$$

Here, \(\lim\limits _{\delta x\rightarrow 0}\frac{\delta y}{\delta x} \) is called the derivative of the function \(f(x)\) at point \(x\) and is denoted by $$\frac{dy}{dx}$$
  • Finding the derivative of a function is called the Differentiation.
Slope at point \(P\), is the derivative of the function at point \(P\). That is \(dy/ dx\).

• Therefore Differentiation is practically used to find the rates of change of something happans. Such as flow of electricity, rates of change of population growth and growth of deceases etc.
