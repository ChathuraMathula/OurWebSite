Integration of the form \(\frac{1}{x}\)

  • Here you will see how to integrate the functions of the form of \(\frac{1}{x}\)

\(\int \frac{1}{x} \ dx\ =\ln \ |x|\ +\ Constant\)

Checking the reverse Process

$$\frac{d(\ln \ |x|)}{dx} \ =\frac{1}{x}$$

Practice Examples


Example 1 $$y\ =\ \frac{4}{x}$$ $$\int y\ dx\ =\int \frac{4}{x} \ dx$$ $$\int y\ dx\ =4\int \frac{1}{x} \ dx$$ $$\int y\ dx\ =4\ln \ |x|\ +\ Constant$$

Example 2 $$y\ =\ \frac{5x+\ 1}{x}$$ $$\int y\ dx\ =\int \left(\frac{5x+\ 1}{x}\right) \ dx$$ $$\int y\ dx\ =\int \left( 5\ +\frac{1}{x}\right) \ dx$$ $$\int y\ dx\ =5x\ +\ \ln \ |x|\ +\ Constant$$

Example 3 $$y\ =\ \frac{5x+\ 1}{x}dx$$ $$find\ \int _{1}^{3} y\ dx$$ $$\int _{1}^{3} y\ dx=\int _{1}^{3}\left(\frac{5x+\ 1}{x}\right) \ dx$$ $$\int _{1}^{3} y\ dx\ =\int _{1}^{3}\left( 5\ +\frac{1}{x}\right) \ dx$$ $$\int _{1}^{3} y\ dx\ =\{5x\ +\ \ln \ |x|\}_{1}^{3}$$ $$\int _{1}^{3} ydx=\{5\times 3+\ln |3|\} -\{5\times 1+\ln |1|\}$$ $$\int _{1}^{3} y\ dx\ =\ \{15\ +\ \ln \ |3|\} -\{5\ +\ 0\}$$ $$\therefore \int _{1}^{3} y\ dx\ =\ 10\ +\ \ln \ |3|$$
