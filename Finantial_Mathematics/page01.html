Simple Interest

❯❯

  • Interest can be expressed in different ways

  • Simple Interest is the most basic type.

  • However It has some issues.

  • Therefore, practically it is limited in usage.

  • It is used in short term investments.

What is Simple Interest?

When an asset(= Capital \(C\)) is given to use over a time period (\(n\)) at some interest rate (\(i\)), the amount of money paid as the interest id given by \(niC\)

Interest = time period(\(n\)) \(\times\) interest rate(\(i\)) \(\times\) Capital(\(C\))
What is the abount of interest you get if you put $ 1000 for a year in a bank that pays simple interest at a rate of 7% per annum (per year) ?

Interest = (\(n\)) \(\times\) (\(i\)) \(\times\) (\(C\)) $$Interest = (1\ year) \times \frac{7}{100} \times $\ 1000$$ $$Interest = $\ 70$$
  • Now you have a total of \($\ 1070\)

If you withdraw this money and deposite for another year in another bank that pays simple interest at the same rate (that is 7% per year)

You get the interest \((I)\)
Interest = (\(n\)) \(\times\) (\(i\)) \(\times\) (\(C\)) $$Interest = (1\ year) \times \frac{7}{100} \times $\ 1070$$ $$Interest = $\ 74.9$$
  • Now you have a total of \($\ 1144.9\)

Now let's compute the total amount of money if you have put your money in the first bank for two years without withdrawing it and depositing in another bank,

The interest \((I)\) you get for 2 years
$$Interest = (2\ year) \times \frac{7}{100} \times $\ 1000$$ $$Interest = $\ 140$$
  • Now you have a total of \($\ 1140\)

You will get that the first method will give you a total of \($\ 1144.9\) while the second method gives \($\ 1140\). $$$\ 1144.9\ >\ $\ 1140$$

Therefore this is the major issue of simple interest when deposite money for a long period of time. Hence, the method compound interest is introduced instead of simple interest to use in the long run.

***The term per annum is in Latin for per year
